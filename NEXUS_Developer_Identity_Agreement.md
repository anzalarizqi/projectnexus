# Developer Identity Agreement
## NEXUS Field Coordination App
### Between: The Developer (identified herein by pseudonym only) and the CSO Partner

---

> **Nature of this document:** This is a good-faith agreement between two parties who have established mutual trust. It is not a legal contract enforceable in a court of law. It is a written record of a shared commitment, intended to protect the developer's identity and legacy, and to give the CSO partner clear guidance on their responsibilities. Both parties sign it not because they fear each other, but because a written agreement survives memory, staff changes, and time.

## Part 2 — What the Developer Has Built and Is Sharing

The developer has built NEXUS — a free, open-source, Android application designed to support lawful civic coordination in Indonesia, particularly in scenarios involving internet shutdowns or active field operations where standard communication tools are unavailable.

The developer built this work:
- Alone, pseudonymously, at personal risk
- Without institutional support, funding, or recognition
- Out of genuine concern for the safety of civil society and the right to peaceful assembly in Indonesia
- With no expectation of profit

The developer is sharing this work with the CSO partner under the terms of this agreement because they trust the CSO partner to steward it responsibly and to protect the developer's identity until disclosure is appropriate.

---

## Part 3 — The CSO Partner's Commitments

By signing this agreement, the CSO partner commits to the following:

### 3.1 Protect the Developer's Identity

The CSO partner will:
- Never publicly disclose, hint at, confirm, or deny the developer's real identity without authorisation under the conditions in Part 4
- Never share the developer's real name, contact information, or any identifying details with any third party — including other CSO staff beyond those who absolutely need to know — without the developer's explicit written consent
- Store the sealed physical copy of this document (containing the developer's real name) in a physically secure location, accessible only to the named CSO representative and their designated successor
- If the CSO representative changes role or leaves the organisation, transfer custodianship of this agreement to a named successor, with the developer's knowledge and informal consent

### 3.2 Manage the App Responsibly

The CSO partner will:
- Distribute the NEXUS APK only to organisations and individuals they personally trust and have vetted
- Not represent NEXUS as having been formally audited unless a formal audit has been completed
- Communicate honestly with users about the security status of the app
- Report critical security issues to the developer promptly via the agreed private communication channel
- Not modify the source code without informing the developer, and not distribute modified versions under the NEXUS name without the developer's consent

### 3.3 Maintain the Security Review Commitment

Before any live operational use of NEXUS, the CSO partner will:
- Refer at least one trusted cybersecurity professional to conduct an informal security review of the app
- Facilitate the review process between the reviewer and the developer (without revealing the developer's identity to the reviewer beyond what is strictly necessary and agreed upon)
- Not proceed with pilot deployment until the developer confirms that critical findings from the review have been addressed

### 3.4 Act as Emergency Contact Recipient

The CSO partner acknowledges that:
- The developer's significant other ([SIGNIFICANT OTHER PSEUDONYM]) has been told to contact the CSO partner in the event of the developer's death or incapacitation
- When such contact is made and confirmed, the CSO partner is authorised to proceed under Condition 1 of Part 4

## Part 4 — When the Developer's Identity May Be Disclosed

The developer's real identity may be publicly disclosed **only** when one or both of the following conditions are met. These conditions are the only authorisation for disclosure. The CSO partner may not disclose the developer's identity for any other reason, regardless of pressure, circumstances, or perceived benefit.

### Condition 1 — Death of the Developer

The developer's real identity may be disclosed after:

1. The CSO partner has been contacted by [SIGNIFICANT OTHER PSEUDONYM] to inform them of the developer's death, AND
2. The CSO partner has verified the death through a death certificate or equivalent official documentation

Upon verification, the CSO partner is authorised — but not obligated — to disclose the developer's identity and their authorship of NEXUS, in a manner and at a time of the CSO partner's choosing, with care and dignity.

The developer's significant other may also be identified at this point if they consent to it.

**What "disclosure" means in practice:** A public statement — through the CSO partner's own communications channels — acknowledging that NEXUS was built by [REAL NAME], an Indonesian developer who chose to remain anonymous during their lifetime to protect themselves and their family. The statement should honour the work and the reason for the anonymity.

### Condition 2 — Developer's Explicit Authorisation

The developer may at any time, for any reason, instruct the CSO partner in writing to disclose their identity. This instruction:
- May come with or without a reason
- May come at the developer's own initiative or in response to a CSO partner request
- Must be in writing (electronic message through the agreed secure channel is sufficient)
- Takes effect immediately upon receipt

The CSO partner may periodically ask the developer whether they wish to authorise disclosure. The developer is under no obligation to do so.

---

## Part 5 — What Happens If the CSO Partner Cannot Continue

If the CSO partner organisation ceases to exist, loses the ability to fulfil this agreement, or otherwise cannot continue as custodian:

1. The CSO representative named in this agreement will make reasonable efforts to notify the developer through the agreed private channel before the situation becomes irreversible
2. The developer and CSO representative will jointly identify a replacement custodian — another trusted individual or organisation — and transfer the sealed physical copy of this document to them
3. If no replacement can be identified and the organisation is dissolving, the CSO representative personally assumes the custodianship obligations of this agreement for a period of at least 12 months, or until a replacement is found

---

## Part 6 — Mutual Confidentiality

Both parties agree to maintain confidentiality about:
- The existence of this agreement
- The nature of their relationship (developer–CSO partner on NEXUS)
- Any details of NEXUS development, distribution, or pilot use that have not been publicly communicated

This confidentiality obligation is mutual. The developer will also not publicly identify the CSO partner as being involved with NEXUS without the CSO partner's consent.

---

## Part 7 — Communication

The developer and CSO partner have agreed on a secure, private communication channel for ongoing contact. This channel is documented separately and known to both parties. All material communications related to this agreement should use this channel.

In the event the developer becomes unreachable through the agreed channel for a period exceeding **90 days** without prior explanation, the CSO partner should:
1. Attempt contact through any secondary channel known to them
2. If still unreachable after a further 30 days, treat this as a possible incapacitation scenario and attempt contact with [SIGNIFICANT OTHER PSEUDONYM]
3. Not take any action regarding identity disclosure based solely on unreachability — only a verified death certificate or explicit authorisation triggers Part 4

---

## Part 8 — The Spirit of This Agreement

This agreement exists because the developer is doing something that matters, at personal risk, without recognition. The CSO partner is accepting a responsibility: to carry the knowledge of who built this, and to release that knowledge at the right time, in the right way.

The developer is not asking for fame. They are asking for two things:
1. That their family is safe while they are alive and building this
2. That when the time comes — whether after their death or by their own choice — the record is set right and their name is attached to work they gave freely for others

The CSO partner's role is to honour that.
